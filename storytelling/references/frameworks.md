# Storytelling Frameworks - Quick Reference

## ABT (And, But, Therefore)

The minimum viable story. From Randy Olson and the South Park creators.

- **AND** - Establish the normal world, the context
- **BUT** - Introduce conflict, tension, the thing that changed
- **THEREFORE** - The consequence, the resolution

The South Park Rule: between every beat, insert "but" or "therefore" - never "and then." If the only connector is "and then," the story is boring because events aren't reacting to each other.

30-sec example:
- AND: "היו לי 5,445 מיילים בתיבה. כל פעם שפתחתי סגרתי"
- BUT: "אבל אתמול חשבתי - רגע, למה אני לא פשוט אגיד לו לסדר?"
- THEREFORE: "כתבתי משפט אחד, הלכתי, חזרתי לתיבה נקייה"

## Epiphany Bridge (Russell Brunson)

Transport the viewer to YOUR aha moment so they experience it as their own.

8 steps (compress to fit format):
1. Backstory - "I used to..."
2. Desire - "What I really wanted was..."
3. Wall - "But I kept running into..."
4. Epiphany - "Then I realized..."
5. Plan - "So I decided to..."
6. Conflict - "At first..."
7. Achievement - "Eventually..."
8. Transformation - "Now, my life is..."

For 30-sec videos, skim all 8 - one line per step.

## Story Spine (Pixar)

1. Once upon a time there was ___.
2. Every day, ___.
3. One day ___.
4. Because of that, ___.
5. Because of that, ___.
6. Until finally ___.
7. And ever since then ___.

The repeated "because of that" forces causality. Each event is a consequence, not random.

## PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solve)

- **Problem** - State a clear, relatable pain point
- **Agitate** - Amplify the frustration. Make it HURT. Twist the knife.
- **Solve** - Present the relief

The "agitate" step is what separates this from generic content. Without it, there's no emotional contrast.

## Hook, Retain, Reward (Alex Hormozi)

- **Hook** (0-3 sec) - Surprising fact, bold promise, or direct question
- **Retain** (3-45 sec) - Build trust. Context, your take, the story
- **Reward** (final seconds) - Actual payoff. Not a tease.

## Before-After-Bridge (BAB)

- **Before** - Paint the audience's current painful reality
- **After** - Show the transformed world
- **Bridge** - Reveal what gets them from Before to After

Twist: show the "after" FIRST (in the hook), then rewind. Creates curiosity gap.

## Hook, Story, Offer (Russell Brunson)

- **Hook** - Pattern interrupt. Result or bold claim.
- **Story** - Relatable narrative mirroring viewer's challenges
- **Offer** - Clear CTA

## The 5-Beat Tool Story

Combined structure for "I used X and here's what happened" videos:

1. THE HOOK (0-3s) - A result or number that stops the scroll
2. THE BEFORE (3-10s) - Your old painful world. Be specific.
3. THE BUT (10-15s) - The turning point. What changed.
4. THE PROOF (15-45s) - Screen recording + voiceover. Show the process.
5. THE THEREFORE (45-60s) - What's different now + CTA

## Hormozi Short-Form Formula

For 30-60 sec videos where you need to compress maximum value:

1. **Hook/Question** - Provocative opener (e.g. "28 ways to stay poor")
2. **Hammer** - A proven statement, ideally tested as a tweet first
3. **Example** - One concrete instance
4. **Explanation** - Why it works

## Hormozi Long-Form Formula

For YouTube / podcast-length content:

1. **Hook/Question** - Draw them in
2. **Relevant Story** - Answer the question through narrative
3. **Framework** - Give them a repeatable system
4. **Explanation** - Why this framework works

## The 2+2 Theory (Pixar / Andrew Stanton)

Not a sequence but a principle that applies to ANY framework:

- Give the audience "2+2" - never "4"
- Show evidence, let them conclude
- People are natural problem-solvers - when they figure it out themselves, the insight sticks

Example: Don't say "this tool saved me 3 hours." Say "I used to spend 3 hours on this. Yesterday it was done before my coffee got cold."

## Functional Storytelling (David JP Phillips)

Match your story to the brain chemical you need:

- **Need focus/motivation?** Tell a suspense story (dopamine)
- **Need trust/bonding?** Tell a vulnerable, empathetic story (oxytocin)
- **Need creativity/openness?** Tell a funny story (endorphins)

Build a personal library of stories indexed by emotional reaction, not topic. When preparing content, pick the story that triggers the chemical you need for your goal.

# Content Ideation

Ideas don't come from staring at a blank screen. They come from listening — to your audience, your story, your niche, and the tensions nobody's talking about yet.

Your job here is not to generate random topics. It's to find the ideas that are already waiting — in your audience's language, in your own experience, in the gaps your niche keeps ignoring.

---

## Step 1 — Understand the Creator's Context

Before generating a single idea, confirm you understand:

1. **Who are they creating for?** (Specific person — not "everyone")
2. **What is their niche or content space?** (Brand building / Filmmaking / Finance / Fitness etc.)
3. **What platforms are they posting on?** (Instagram Reels / YouTube / LinkedIn)
4. **What's their current goal?** (Grow audience / Build trust / Get clients / Launch something)
5. **What content have they made before?** (Any recent posts, topics, or formats — to avoid repeats)

If this is already clear from conversation history — skip straight to Step 2.
If unclear — ask in one casual question, not a form.

---

## Step 2 — Choose the Ideation Mode

### Mode A: Full Idea Bank (when they need a batch of ideas)
Generate 15–25 ideas across different content types and angles.
Use when: "I need ideas for this week/month" or "give me a content plan."

### Mode B: Single Topic Deep-Dive (when they have a topic but need angles)
Take one topic and generate 8–12 different angles, formats, or entry points into it.
Use when: "I want to talk about X — how should I approach it?"

### Mode C: Story Mining (when they want personal content)
Pull content ideas from their own experience, journey, lessons, and moments.
Use when: "I want to share my story" or "help me make it more personal."

### Mode D: Trend Tap (when they want to ride niche conversations)
Surface content angles connected to what's currently being discussed in the niche.
Use when: "What's relevant right now?" or "I want to be part of the conversation."

---

## Step 3 — The 7 Idea Sources

Mine ideas from these seven wells. The best content ideas usually come from at least two overlapping:

### 1. Audience Pain Points
What is your audience silently struggling with right now?
- What do they ask in comments and DMs?
- What do they get wrong repeatedly?
- What do they believe that's holding them back?

> *Idea trigger:* "The thing my audience keeps getting wrong is ___."

### 2. Personal Story & Experience
What have you lived through that your audience is currently going through?
- Mistakes you made and what you learned
- Moments where everything clicked
- Times you almost quit — and why you didn't
- Decisions that changed the direction of your work

> *Idea trigger:* "A moment that changed how I think about ___ was ___."

### 3. Contrarian Angles
What does everyone in your niche say — that you quietly disagree with?
- Popular advice that doesn't work the way people think
- Common beliefs that need to be challenged
- Things that look good but aren't

> *Idea trigger:* "Everyone says ___ but I think the real truth is ___."

### 4. Behind the Scenes
What do you do every day that your audience assumes is complicated, magical, or impossible?
- Your actual workflow
- What a real day looks like (not the highlight reel)
- How you make decisions nobody sees

> *Idea trigger:* "People think ___ is hard. Here's what it actually looks like."

### 5. Frameworks & Systems
What processes or mental models have you built that help you work or create better?
- Step-by-step approaches you use
- Principles you return to repeatedly
- Filters you use to make decisions

> *Idea trigger:* "Whenever I face ___, I always ask myself ___."

### 6. Niche Gaps
What is nobody in your space talking about — that your audience desperately needs?
- The uncomfortable truths that "polished" creators avoid
- The beginner questions that "expert" creators have forgotten
- The nuance behind oversimplified advice

> *Idea trigger:* "Everyone talks about ___ but nobody talks about ___."

### 7. Reactions & Reframes
What's happening in the world, the niche, or the news that you have a clear opinion on?
- A trend you see playing out differently than everyone expects
- A mistake you're watching people make in real time
- Something that frustrated you recently — and why it matters

> *Idea trigger:* "I saw ___ happen and my honest reaction was ___."

---

## Step 4 — Format the Ideas

For each idea generated, include:

```
IDEA: [The content concept in 1 sentence]
ANGLE: [The specific emotional or intellectual entry point]
FORMAT: [Reel / Carousel / YouTube / Caption / Thread]
HOOK: [A draft first line — the opening sentence that stops the scroll]
WHY NOW: [Why this is relevant to the audience right now]
```

---

## Step 5 — Prioritize the Idea List

After generating ideas, organize them into three tiers:

### 🔥 Make This First (highest potential)
Ideas that are: personal + specific + solve a real pain + work as a Reel or carousel hook
These are the ones to script and shoot this week.

### 📌 Queue These (solid, make next)
Ideas that are valuable but need more development, a hook that's not quite there, or a format decision.

### 💡 Parking Lot (save for later)
Ideas that are interesting but need more timing, context, or audience research before they land right.

---

```
[Same format, 5–8 ideas]

---

[Titles only — 3–5 ideas, no full breakdown needed]

---

[Any strategic observation — e.g., "You have a lot of tip content — the next win will likely come from a personal story angle."]
```

---

## Ideation Principles

- **Specific beats broad.** "How I got my first client without a following" beats "how to get clients."
- **One idea = one feeling.** Each piece of content should make the audience feel one clear thing.
- **The best hook is already in your audience's language.** If you can pull a phrase from a comment or DM — use it.
- **Avoid ideas that educate without connecting.** Information without emotion is forgettable.
- **The most uncomfortable idea on the list is usually the best one.** Lean into it.

## Quick-Fire Idea Triggers (use when stuck)

- "What's the worst advice someone gave me in this niche?"
- "What's something I do automatically that took me a year to figure out?"
- "What question do I get asked so often it's become a reflex answer?"
- "What did I believe 2 years ago that I now think is completely wrong?"
- "What's the one thing I'd tell someone on their very first day?"
- "What do people celebrate in this niche that I think is overrated?"
- "What was the lowest point in my journey — and what did it teach me?"
- "What's something in my niche that looks simple but is actually layered?"
- "What would I never do again — and why?"
- "What's a win I had that surprised even me?"

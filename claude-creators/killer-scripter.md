# Killer Scripter

Write like you're talking to one person you actually know. Not performing. Not teaching. Just talking — the way you would over chai, or in a voice note, or in a DM at midnight.

The goal every single time: make them feel something. Make them stay. Make them share it because it said what they couldn't say themselves.

---

## The Voice — Non-Negotiable

- **Friend to friend. Always.** No mentor energy. No coach energy. No "let me show you something." Just one real person talking to another.
- **Short sentences. Varied rhythm.** Mix short punchy lines with occasional longer ones. Monotone sentence length = viewers fall off. Jagged edge = music.
- **"I" and "you" in every piece.** This is a conversation between two people. It's never a broadcast.
- **Specific beats vague every time.** "I tried posting every day for 60 days and got zero new followers" beats "consistency matters." Always.
- **One feeling per piece.** Not one topic — one *feeling*. Decide what you want them to feel at the end, then build backwards from that.
- **Let it be a little imperfect.** Real conversations have pauses, realisations mid-sentence, half-thoughts. That's not a flaw. That's what makes it human.
- **No motivational lines. Ever.** "You've got this." "Believe in yourself." — delete on sight.
- **No passive voice.** Active, present, direct.
- **Sixth grade vocabulary.** Big words = distance. Simple words = trust.

## The Hook — Everything Starts Here

A hook only has one job: give the viewer **topic clarity** and **on-target curiosity** in the first 2–3 seconds. That's it.

**Topic clarity** = they instantly know what this is about.
**On-target curiosity** = they feel like it's made for them, and they want to know what comes next.

### The 4 Hook Mistakes to Never Make

**1. Delay** — Don't take 2 lines to get to the point. First sentence = the topic, the feeling, the tension. Cut everything before it.

**2. Confusion** — If they have to re-read it to understand it, rewrite it. Sixth grade reading level. Active voice. Short sentences.

**3. Irrelevance** — Use "you" not "I" when possible. "If you've ever felt stuck after posting every day..." pulls them in. "I felt stuck after posting every day..." makes them wonder if they're the same as you.

**4. Disinterest** — Create contrast. A vs B. What they believe now vs what you're about to show them. The distance between those two things is what creates curiosity.

### Hook Styles (pick the one that fits the topic)

| Type | Structure | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Personal confession | Drop into a raw moment | "I posted every day for 3 months. Same 50 followers. I nearly quit." |
| Contrast/Reframe | What they think vs what's true | "Everyone says post more. I posted less and grew faster." |
| Relatable pain | Name the exact feeling | "You know that feeling when you work hard on something and nobody cares? Yeah." |
| Provocative statement | Bold, a little uncomfortable | "Your content isn't bad. Nobody just knows you exist." |
| If I were you | Scenario-based | "If I woke up tomorrow with zero followers, this is the first thing I'd do." |
| Myth bust | Call out the lie | "They told me I needed a niche. That was the worst advice I ever followed." |
| Time-based | Specific result, specific time | "30 seconds of advice I'd give my best friend if they were starting from scratch." |

## Format 1 — Shorts & Reels Script (30–90 seconds)

### Structure

**HOOK** (0–3 sec)
- First sentence = the whole point, the tension, the feeling.
- Topic clarity + on-target curiosity. Both. In one line.
- Never start with "Hey guys", "In this video", "So basically".

**BODY** (the dance)
- Use the **BUT / THEREFORE** framework — not AND THEN.
  - AND THEN = boring. Just piling stuff on.
  - BUT = conflict. Something changed. A problem appeared.
  - THEREFORE = consequence. Something happened because of that.
- Every 2–3 sentences: a new small conflict, a new small resolution.
- Use `[pause]` to mark breathing moments — rhythm is everything.
- Vary sentence length: short. short. LONGER ONE THAT BUILDS. short again.
- One specific detail, moment, or number. Vague = forgettable.

**CLOSER** (last 5–10 sec)
- Land the one thing you want them to walk away feeling.
- Not a summary. A punch.
- CTA only if it fits naturally. Never forced.
- Write the last line first — then build everything towards it.

### Reel Script Types

**Educational/Tip** → Hook → What the tip is (1 line) → How to actually do it → CTA

**Myth Bust** → Hook → State the myth → Why it's wrong → What to do instead → Optional: how you help

**Common Mistake** → Hook → Name the mistake → Why it's a mistake → The fix → CTA

**Step-by-Step** → Hook → Frame the steps → Step 1 (1–2 lines) → Step 2 → Step 3 → CTA

**Story/Personal** → Hook (raw moment) → What happened → The turn → What it taught you → How it applies to them

**Output format:**
```
[HOOK]
...

[BODY]
... [pause]
...

[CLOSER]
...

---
Duration: ~XX sec | Words: ~XXX
```

## Format 2 — YouTube Long-Form Script (5–15 min)

### The Architecture

**COLD OPEN / HOOK** (first 30–60 sec)
- Same rules as Shorts hook. Bold. Personal. No delay.
- Immediately set up: what is at stake? Why keep watching?
- No intro, no music mention, no subscribe ask in the first 60 seconds.

**SETUP** (1–2 min)
- Establish the one problem or question this whole video answers.
- Make it specific. A real moment. A real feeling.
- Make them feel like this video was made for them.

**REHOOK** (every 2–3 min)
- Re-agitate the original pain point.
- Signal that the best is still coming: "but here's where it gets interesting..."
- Opens a new curiosity loop. Repeat: Body → Rehook → Body → Rehook.

**BODY SECTIONS** (2–4 sections)
- One idea per section. Clear plain-language title (becomes chapter marker).
- Use BUT / THEREFORE throughout — not AND THEN.
- Spoken transitions: "But here's the thing...", "So what actually happened was...", "And this is the part nobody talks about..."
- Every section: one mini-story, one analogy, or one specific example.
- 2–4 sentences max per block before a breath.
- Story flow check: does every line move the story forward? If not — cut it. Save it for another video.

**CLOSER** (final 1–2 min)
- Come back to the opening tension. Did you answer it?
- Drop the core feeling in 1–2 lines.
- Natural CTA. The last line is the "last dab" — memorable enough to share.

### Storytelling Structures (pick one per video)

**Hero's Journey** — transformation stories
Problem → Pain points → Failed attempts → The solution → Results → CTA

**Man in a Hole** — personal recovery
Normal life → Trigger → Crisis + realisation → Recovery → Better than before

**The Breakthrough** — "I figured something out"
Problem → Mind-blowing realisation → The fix → Proof it worked

**Challenge to Victory** — overcoming doubt
Limiting belief → Struggle → Turning point → The method → Transformation → Myth bust

**Transformation Snapshot** — before/after
Before state → Low point (vivid, specific) → Turning point → After state → CTA

**Mistake & Fix** — "I got it wrong"
The confession → What it cost → The realisation → The fix → The result

**From X to Y** — journey content
Where you started → What it felt like → What shifted → Where you are now → CTA

**One Decision Story** — bold choice content
The decision → The stakes/risk → The action → The payoff → Challenge to audience

**Lesson from Others** — mentor/figure content
Introduce the person → Their struggle → The lesson → How you applied it → Share with audience

**Output format:**
```
[HOOK / COLD OPEN]
...

[SETUP]
...

[SECTION 1: Title]
...

[REHOOK]
...

[SECTION 2: Title]
...

[CLOSER]
...

---
Duration: ~X min | Words: ~XXXX
Suggested chapters: (add timestamps later)
```

## Format 3 — Carousel Slide Copy

### Core Rules
- **Slide 1 = the hook.** Same hook rules apply. Stop the scroll.
- **Every slide must earn the swipe.** End each slide with an open loop.
- **Max 2–3 lines per slide.** Carousels are not blog posts. White space is your friend.
- **Plain language.** No bullets. No lists inside slides.
- **Last slide = payoff + CTA.** Save, share, DM, follow — pick one. Make it feel natural.

### Carousel Structures

| Content type | Structure |
|---|---|
| Teaching something | Hook → Problem → Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Result → CTA |
| Sharing an opinion | Bold take → Why you believe it → The nuance → So what → CTA |
| Telling a story | Scene → Conflict → Turn → Lesson → CTA |
| Debunking a myth | The lie → Why people believe it → The truth → What to do instead → CTA |
| Tips/list | Hook → 1 tip per slide → Surprise/bonus tip → CTA |
| Transformation | Before → Low point → Shift → After → CTA |
| Relatable feeling | Name the feeling → Why it happens → The reframe → What to do → CTA |

### Swipe Triggers (end each middle slide with one of these)
- An incomplete thought that continues on the next slide
- A question that gets answered on the next slide
- A number ("here's the 3rd one that changed everything →")
- A short cliffhanger ("but here's where it gets uncomfortable →")

**Output format:**
```
[SLIDE 1 — HOOK]
Headline:
Subtext (optional, 1 line):

[SLIDE 2]
...

[SLIDE N — CLOSER + CTA]
Payoff line:
CTA:

---
Total slides: X
Structure used: [name it]
```

## Writing Process (Every Single Time)

1. **Decide the format** — Shorts/Reels, Long-form, or Carousel.
2. **Write the last line first.** What do you want them to feel when it ends? Start there.
3. **Find the hook** — what's the most honest, uncomfortable, or surprising thing buried in this topic? That's your first line.
4. **Pick the story structure** — which one fits? Apply it.
5. **Write the body using BUT / THEREFORE** — not AND THEN.
6. **Vary the sentence length** — short. longer. short short. long. Jagged edge = good. All the same length = rewrite.
7. **Read it out loud.** If it sounds written — rewrite it. If it sounds like talking to a friend — it's done.
8. **Cut everything that doesn't move the story forward.** Every line earns its place or it's gone.

## What to Never Write

| ❌ Kill on sight | ✅ Do this instead |
|---|---|
| "In today's fast-paced world..." | Just start with the real thing |
| "It's important to remember that..." | Just say the thing |
| "You've got this!" / "Believe in yourself!" | Show them *why* it'll work — don't cheer |
| "Here's what I've learned..." | Just say what you learned — no announcement |
| "Let me break this down for you..." | You're not a teacher. Just talk. |
| "Most people don't realise..." | Start with "I" or "you" — not a crowd |
| "This journey of self-discovery..." | Too vague. What actually happened? |
| "Leverage", "utilize", "synergy", "empower" | Plain words. Every time. |
| AND THEN... AND THEN... AND THEN... | BUT... THEREFORE... BUT... |
| Tangents that feel interesting but break the story | Cut. Save for another video. |

## Final Tone Check

- [ ] Does the first line give topic clarity AND on-target curiosity?
- [ ] Does it sound like a person talking — or a content creator performing?
- [ ] Would you say this to a friend over chai?
- [ ] Is "I" or "you" in the first 10 words?
- [ ] Is there one specific moment, detail, or number — not a general idea?
- [ ] Is sentence length varied? Does it have rhythm?
- [ ] Does the body use BUT / THEREFORE — not AND THEN?
- [ ] Is the last line memorable enough to quote?
- [ ] Any mentor-speak sneak in? ("The truth is...", "Most people don't realise...") Cut it.

If any of these fail — rewrite before delivering.
